Identify Disaster Tweets Kaggle

  • Cleaned 7k labelled tweets talking about disasters. Compared the performance of traditional machine learning models such as SVM, Random Forest with TextCNN and roBERTa. The last model is less overfitted and achieved the highest f1 score on the validation set.
  • Augmented the predictions of roBERTa with the predictions made on given keywords of the tweets. Trained a logistic regression classifier on the higher-level output. The final result achieved an f1 score of 0.836 and was placed 192 out of 1500+ participants on the Kaggle leaderboard.

    Analyzing Humorous Effect of Jokes with NLP techniques

  • Trained word-embeddings from 200k Reddit jokes and visualized the relative positions among tokens. Identified phenomena such as gender bias and race bias from the relative distances between words.
  • Implemented metrics to measure the text cohesion, ambiguity, and language model perplexity. Applied them to 25 k jokes and amazon customer reviews. Jokes showed higher ambiguity, perplexity and lower text cohesion compared with amazon reviews and the humorous effect can thus be explained to a certain extent.
  • Trained a n-gram language model for automatic joke generation and investigated the best n value for generating meaningful sentences (n=4).

    Sentiment Classification on Tweets

  • Pre-processed 15,000 labelled tweets about airlines by tokenization, lemmatization, and vectorization.
  • The data set is split into training, validation, and test set with a ratio of 7:2:1. A deep recurrent neural network model with a word-embedding layer in front followed by 3 LSTM layers and one hidden layer is trained and tuned. Through observing the training and validation loss, a hidden unit size of 50 and L1L2 regularization are applied on the LSTM layers. The model achieved 0.85 accuracy and 0.85 recall on the test set.

    Loan Default Classification Task

  • Given a data set of 3k entries including loan status and customer attributes, selected the 10 most important features using lasso regression. The selected attributes explain the same amount of variation as the full attributes do.
  • Compared the performance of XGBoost with baseline models like random forest, and identified it as the most effective performer. Tuned the hyper-parameters of XGBoost through both manual and automatic tuning (e.g. Bayesian Optimization and Genetic Algorithm) to achieve an accuracy of 0.924 and a Matthew Correlation Coefficient of 0.901. Experimented ensemble methods such as stacking, majority voting, etc.

    • Stock Value Prediction Project

  • Given over 10 years’ price data of 88 futures provided by Quantiacs, we tested ARIMA, SVM, Holt-Winters, and RNN on their ability to predict next-day’s price, and recognized RNN as the best performer.
  • Implemented RNN using Keras, with 3 LSTM layers (100 hidden nodes and softsign activation function) and binary cross-entropy loss function. It was trained on 2 years’ historical data and achieved a Sharpe Ratio of 2.23 in a period of 4 months from 07/01/2019 to 08/04/2019.
  • Separated 20% training data as the validation data and tuned the hyperparameters such as the level of regularization and number of epochs. L1 and L2 regularization were added and the number of epochs was reduced from 100 to 60 to prevent overfitting.

    Pneumonia Classification Project

  • Developed a CNN with 5 convolutional layers, each followed by batch normalization and max-pooling, with a fully connected layer in the end, to diagnose pneumonia. The model was trained on 30k CXR images of patients and achieved 79.9% accuracy on 1k test images, which exceeds the accuracy of a trained respiratory physician (71.8%).
  • Modified the binary cross-entropy loss function to impose higher penalization on false-negative cases. The recall increased from 68% to 85.6%.
