Analyzing Humorous Effect of Jokes with NLP techniques

  • Trained word-embeddings from 200k Reddit jokes and visualized the relative positions among tokens. Identified phenomena such as gender bias and race bias from the relative distances between words.
  • Implemented metrics to measure the text cohesion, ambiguity, and language model perplexity. Applied them to 25 k jokes and amazon customer reviews. Jokes showed higher ambiguity, perplexity and lower text cohesion compared with amazon reviews and the humorous effect can thus be explained to a certain extent.
  • Trained a n-gram language model for automatic joke generation and investigated the best n value for generating meaningful sentences (n=4).

    Sentiment Classification on Tweets

  • Pre-processed 15,000 labelled tweets about airlines by tokenization, lemmatization, and vectorization.
  • The data set is split into training, validation, and test set with a ratio of 7:2:1. A deep recurrent neural network model with a word-embedding layer in front followed by 3 LSTM layers and one hidden layer is trained and tuned. Through observing the training and validation loss, a hidden unit size of 50 and L1L2 regularization are applied on the LSTM layers. The model achieved 0.85 accuracy and 0.85 recall on the test set.
